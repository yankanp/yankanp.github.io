Accessibility XR / VR Human-Centered Design Research + Prototyping
I design accessible immersive experiences and build prototypes that ship.

I’m a Computer Science PhD candidate at the University of Rhode Island (ASSET Lab), working on accessibility and immersive technology. My research focuses on making VR usable and empowering for adults with intellectual and developmental disabilities (I/DD), and translating insights into practical design guidance and deployed applications. I have the privilege of being guided by Prof. Krishna Venkatasubramanian and also been lucky to collaborate with Dr. Tina-Marie Ranalli

Selected work

A quick tour of projects recruiters ask about

VR Accessibility for Adults with I/DD (DIS 2025)

Designed and conducted an accessibility-focused VR study and synthesized findings into actionable design recommendations. Communicated outcomes through clear storytelling and visual presentations to support practitioners and researchers.

Accessibility VR Qualitative Study Design Recommendations

R3: Recognize, Report, Respond (Shipped)

Participatory-design mobile app that teaches adults with I/DD about abuse awareness and reporting. Built for real-world use and released on major app stores.

Participatory Design Flutter Trauma-informed Deployment

S2: Self-Care & Support (Shipped)

Co-designed and developed a mobile app to help adults with I/DD self-regulate post-trauma using guided exercises and motivational prompts.

Self-care Flutter Accessibility

Fintech Microservices + DevOps (Industry)

Designed and developed microservices for a fintech application; improved API performance by ~40% via profiling and caching. Deployed services with Docker/Kubernetes and CI/CD (Jenkins) across multiple environments.

Java/Spring Microservices Kubernetes CI/CD
